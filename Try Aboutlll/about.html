About Us

Welcome to Pinoy Foodies, your go-to destination for authentic and delicious Filipino dishes. Our mission is to share the rich and diverse culinary heritage of the Philippines with food lovers around the world.

Whether you're craving classic adobo, hearty sinigang, or festive lechon, we offer step-by-step recipes, cooking tips, and cultural insights to make your cooking experience enjoyable and rewarding.

Filipino cuisine reflects a unique blend of indigenous flavors and influences from Spanish, Chinese, American, and other Asian cultures, resulting in a rich fusion of taste and tradition.

Our team is passionate about preserving culinary heritage and helping the next generation of cooks discover the joy of Filipino home cooking.

Tinolang Manok

Ingredients: Chicken, green papaya, ginger, garlic, onion, chili leaves, fish sauce, water.

Procedure: Sauté ginger, garlic, and onion. Add chicken and cook until lightly browned. Add water, bring to boil, add papaya. Simmer until tender. Add chili leaves and fish sauce. Serve hot.

Tortang Talong

Ingredients: Eggplant, eggs, salt, pepper, oil.

Procedure: Grill eggplant until skin is charred. Peel and flatten. Dip in beaten eggs. Fry until golden brown.

Ginisang Munggo

Ingredients: Mung beans, garlic, onion, tomato, spinach/malunggay, pork (optional), fish sauce.

Procedure: Boil mung beans until soft. Sauté aromatics and pork. Add beans and fish sauce. Simmer and add greens. Serve hot.

Pinakbet

Ingredients: Eggplant, okra, bitter melon, squash, string beans, tomatoes, shrimp paste.

Procedure: Sauté garlic, onion, and tomatoes. Add vegetables and shrimp paste. Simmer until cooked but still crisp.

Pandesal

Ingredients: Flour, yeast, sugar, salt, oil, breadcrumbs.

Procedure: Mix dry ingredients, add oil/water. Knead, rise, shape, coat in breadcrumbs, rise again, bake.

These meals require minimal ingredients and are perfect for everyday cooking. Discover how simple ingredients can turn into flavorful meals that bring people together.
