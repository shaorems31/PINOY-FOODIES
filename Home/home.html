Tapsilog

A classic Filipino breakfast with tapa (cured beef), sinangag (garlic rice), and itlog (egg).

Intro: Tapsilog is a classic Filipino breakfast combining savory beef tapa, garlic fried rice (sinangag), and a sunny-side-up egg (itlog). It’s a filling and flavorful meal loved for its simplicity and satisfying taste.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp calamansi juice or lemon juice
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • Cooked garlic fried rice (sinangag)
  • Fried eggs (1 per serving)
  • Cooking oil

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the beef in soy sauce, calamansi juice, garlic, sugar, and pepper for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and pan-fry the marinated beef until browned and cooked through.
  3. Prepare garlic fried rice and fry eggs to your liking.
  4. Plate everything together and serve hot.

Nutrition Info: Approx. 500-600 kcal per serving

Pro Tip: For extra flavor, add a vinegar dipping sauce on the side.

Tocilog

Sweet cured pork (tocino) served with garlic rice and a sunny-side-up egg.

Intro: Tocilog is a beloved Filipino breakfast dish consisting of tocino (sweet cured pork), sinangag (garlic rice), and itlog (fried egg). It’s a perfect blend of sweet, savory, and hearty flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb tocino (store-bought or homemade)
  • Cooked garlic fried rice (sinangag)
  • Fried eggs (1 per serving)
  • Water (for cooking tocino)
  • Cooking oil

Instructions:

  1. Place tocino in a pan with a bit of water and cook until water evaporates.
  2. Once dry, add oil and pan-fry the tocino until caramelized.
  3. Prepare garlic rice and fry eggs.
  4. Serve together on a plate and enjoy!

Nutrition Info: Approx. 550 kcal per serving

Pro Tip: Add pickled papaya (atchara) on the side to balance the sweetness.

Pandesal

Soft and slightly sweet bread rolls, perfect with coffee or cheese spread.

Intro: Pandesal is the quintessential Filipino bread roll—soft, slightly sweet, and coated with breadcrumbs. It’s a staple in Filipino households, often enjoyed for breakfast or merienda.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2¼ tsp active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil or butter
  • Breadcrumbs for coating

Instructions:

  1. Dissolve yeast in warm milk with a pinch of sugar. Let it foam (5-10 mins).
  2. Combine all ingredients into a dough and knead until smooth.
  3. Let the dough rise for 1–1.5 hours until doubled.
  4. Shape into small rolls, coat in breadcrumbs, and let rise again (30 mins).
  5. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 15–20 minutes until golden brown.

Nutrition Info: Approx. 150–180 kcal per roll

Pro Tip: Serve warm with butter, cheese, or dipped in hot coffee!
